Egypt's airports.. 6 years of global achievements


President Abdel-Fattah El-Sisi, since assuming power in Egypt in 2014, has put in place a comprehensive plan and a clear vision for sustainable development 2030, which is a roadmap that aims to develop all sectors of the state and make the most of their potential. During the past six years, the Egyptian state has paid great attention to the civil aviation sector, which is a major focus. In a development plan that puts Egypt on the global map. Many clear achievements have been achieved since 2014 until now in the civil aviation sector, whether through developing and raising the efficiency of many airports or opening new ones, modernizing and raising the efficiency of navigation and air control systems and enhancing security measures, which was reflected in Egypt's presence in a distinguished position among companies and international institutions. The number of Egyptian airports is 27 international and domestic, including two airports with the B.O.T system, Marsa Alam and El Alamein Airport. The capacity of Egyptian airports is about 30 million passengers annually. During the era of President El-Sisi, 5 new airports came to light in record time, as the trial operation of Sphinx Airport was opened in the 6th of October City in October 2018, and the airport received the first international plane in February 2019, and it cost 300 million pounds, and it is located on an area of 25 thousand and 500 square meters and aims to reduce pressure. at Cairo Airport. The trial operation of the Capital International Airport was inaugurated in July 2019 on an area of 16 square kilometers, and it was designed and constructed according to the highest international standards and based on Egyptian expertise only and national companies. In January 2019, President El-Sisi inaugurated Bernice International Airport, which facilitates linking Cairo with cities in the south, especially the cities of Halayeb and Shalateen, and enhances the increase in tourism in remote Red Sea cities such as Marsa Alam, at a cost of 2.310 billion pounds. In October 2020, President Abdel Fattah El-Sisi witnessed the inauguration of a number of projects, including the Bardawil International Airport, which is located on an area of 11 km in central Sinai in the Al-Maliz area to serve industrial areas, including cement factories, marble quarries, agricultural areas, and fishing areas throughout Sinai. At a cost of 860 million pounds. The president agreed to allocate a land area for the construction of a new airport in Ras Sidr, at a cost of 3 billion pounds. The project includes the construction of a marina, which will make it an attraction area that extends to the city of Sharm el-Sheikh. The airport will be under a BOT system on a land area of about 34 million square meters, in addition to an integrated tourism development on an area of 6 million square meters in the North Oyoun Moussa area in Ras Sidr, Sinai. During the era of President El-Sisi, the airports of Burj Al-Arab, Sharm El-Sheikh, Hurghada, Aswan, Luxor and Port Said were upgraded, navigation and air control systems were upgraded, and security measures were strengthened at all airports. The cost of radar coverage of the airspace amounted to about 305 million dollars, and a satellite network was established for 22 satellite stations and 8 microwave lines to ensure the safety of the Egyptian airspace at a cost of 10.2 million euros, in addition to 2.3 billion pounds to develop insurance requirements for all Egyptian airports.










Cairo International Airport

It is an international airport located about 22 kilometers away from the center of Cairo, the capital of Egypt, in the northeastern direction. The airport’s land area is about 40 million square meters. It is considered the air gateway to Egypt and the continent of Africa.



Sphinx International Airport

It is an international airport established in 2016 in Giza, west of Cairo, in Egypt. It is located on the Cairo-Alexandria Desert Road at kilometer 45. The airport was established with the aim of relieving pressure from Cairo International Airport and lifting pressure and density from the heart of Cairo.

Aswan International Airport

The airport is located in the city of Aswan, in southern Egypt. It is an international tourist airport, serving the Nubia region in southern Egypt, in addition to Abu Simbel Airport, about 10 km away from the city, and it is part of the Aswan Military Air Base.

Assiut International Airport

It is an international airport located in Assiut Governorate, in southern Egypt.

Luxor International Airport

It is the main airport that serves the city of Luxor, located 6 km east of the city, and in May 1946 the Egyptian Civil Aviation Authority received this airport, the air control center and the meteorological station from the British military authority, and because of the importance of this airport because it is located in the most important antiquities area in the world, the authority It is undergoing successive development operations in order to prepare it to receive tourists from all over the world

Borg El Arab International Airport

An Egyptian international airport is located in the city of Alexandria, with an area of about 8,520 acres (34.47 km²), about 49 km from the city center of Alexandria in the southwest direction, and about 14 km from the new city of Burj Al Arab in the eastern direction. The airport handled more than 2.2 million passengers, with a total of 20,195 domestic and international flights in 2019.

Taba International Airport

And an international airport located on the Sinai plateau, at an altitude of 2470 feet. The airport has only one gate from which charter flights operate mainly. Taba VOR - Taba Wireless Platform (ID: TBA) is located 0.43 nautical miles from the threshold of runway 22.s

Marsa Matrouh International Airport

And an international airport serving the city of Marsa Matruh, the airport also includes a military base. It is about 2 km away from the city in the southwestern direction, and it is among the airports that the British army handed over to the aviation authority in 1954. EgyptAir has been operating regular flights to it since the beginning of the fifties during the summer seasons

Sharm El-Sheikh International Airport

It is an international airport 23 km away from Sharm El-Sheikh, formerly known as Afira International Airport, and it is one of the giant tourist airports. The Egyptian Civil Aviation Authority (currently the Ministry of Civil Aviation) took over it after recovering the rest of the occupied lands from Sinai, in April 1982, to manage it civilly. It had a simple, prefabricated building divided into a hall for travel and another for arrival, each with a capacity of 150 passengers / hour, and an apron for 4 medium aircraft.

Hurghada International Airport

And an international airport about 4 km away from Hurghada, and the civil airport is located within the scope of the Hurghada military base. In May 1966, the state allocated 102 thousand pounds to prepare Hurghada Airport to receive jet planes carrying tourist groups in response to the desire of tourism companies to land planes coming from Europe directly instead of From landing at Cairo International Airport and then traveling to Hurghada, but after the 1967 war, the airport was allocated for the war effort until victory was achieved in the October War in 1973 AD. With the beginning of the peace talks, the Authority in November 1977 AD prepared and operated part of this airport for civil aviation flights
